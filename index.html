About Me

Research Experience

As a UPRM student, I became a member of the CMS experiment and quickly began acclimating to the software and analysis tools environment. The CMS experiment is one of the main experiments at the LHC at CERN, a prestigious research laboratory of particle physics based at Geneva, Switzerland. I've been working remotely on Supersymmetry Analysis, with a focus on the search for new physics targeting final states with one or more top quarks. Furthermore, I have had to train a top tagging algorithm that implements a combination of three approaches to top tagging in order to get the best efficiency over the largest range of top transverse momentum. Currently I’m working to plot basic variables and histograms of the HT and Nt distributions after the baseline selection and of the MET distributions. I’ve have to face some roadblocks but soon I’ll focus on Machine Learning for SUSY.
